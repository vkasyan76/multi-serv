# Booking And Calendar Help

## calendar-provider-add-availability

Audience: provider.

Providers manage availability from their dashboard calendar.

Available calendar slots can be created, moved, resized, or removed by the provider while they are still available.

## calendar-available-future-slots

Audience: customer.

Customers can select available future slots on a provider page.

Past available slots are not shown for booking, and booked or completed slots cannot be selected as available time.

## calendar-slot-limit

Audience: customer.

A customer can select up to 8 slots per booking.

If the selection limit is reached, the user should clear a selected slot before adding another one.

## calendar-slot-statuses

Audience: customer and provider.

Calendar/service status and payment status are separate.

Available means a slot can be selected by a customer. Requested means the customer submitted a booking request and the provider has not confirmed it yet. Requested slots are blocked from other customers while the request is pending. Scheduled means the provider confirmed the request and the booked service is planned. Completed means the provider has marked the service completed. Accepted means the customer accepted completion. Disputed means the customer reported a problem with the completed service.

Payment status is separate and may show whether a booking or order is unpaid, pending, due, or paid.

## calendar-requested-slots

Audience: customer and provider.

A booking request is not yet a scheduled booking. The provider must confirm the request before it becomes scheduled.

Requested slots are blocked from other customers while the request is pending. Other customers should only see those times as unavailable; they should not see private request details.

The customer who created the request can see it in their Orders page as awaiting provider confirmation. Providers can see requested bookings in their dashboard and can confirm or decline them.

## calendar-no-slots

Audience: customer.

If no slots are available for a selected day or week, the user can check another day or week, contact the provider about availability, or check back later.

Support chat cannot create slots or inspect the provider's private calendar.

## calendar-slot-disappeared

Audience: customer.

A selected slot may become unavailable if it was booked by someone else, changed, removed, or is no longer in the future.

If this happens, the user should refresh availability and choose another available slot.

## calendar-why-2300-disabled

Audience: customer and provider.

The app prevents starting a 1-hour slot at 23:00 because that would cross midnight. The last 1-hour slot starts at 22:00.

## calendar-customer-contact-provider

Audience: customer.

Customers may need to sign in to contact a provider.

For service execution questions, the provider is usually the primary counterpart, except where platform dispute handling applies.
